Biryani Recipe

Biryani is a rich, aromatic rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent and is loved across regions for its bold flavors and fragrant spices. It is typically made by layering long-grain basmati rice with marinated meat—such as chicken, mutton, or fish—or vegetables, along with a blend of spices like saffron, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves.

Biryani is prepared by first marinating meat (such as chicken or mutton) in a mixture of yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and fresh herbs like mint and coriander. Meanwhile, basmati rice is partially cooked with whole spice s such as cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and bay leaves until it is about 70% done. Fried onions are also prepared separately, adding a sweet, caramelized flavor that is essential to the dish.

To assemble, the marinated meat is layered with the partially cooked rice in a heavy-bottomed pot. Saffron soaked in milk, ghee, fried onions, and herbs are sprinkled between the layers. The pot is then sealed and cooked on low heat using the “dum” method, allowing the rice to fully cook and absorb the rich flavors of the meat and spices. Once done, the biryani is gently mixed and served hot, often accompanied by raita or salan for balance.

Ingredients

  • Basmati Rice
  • Chicken
  • Curd
  • Vegetables
  • Ginger-Garlic paste
  • Ghee

Steps

  1. Mix chicken, mutton, or vegetables with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, salt, lemon juice, and chopped mint and coriander leaves. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (longer for better flavor).
  2. Wash and soak basmati rice for about 20–30 minutes. Boil water with salt and whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaf), then cook the rice until it is about 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Slice onions thinly and fry them in oil or ghee until golden brown and crispy. Set aside for layering.
  4. In a heavy pot, cook the marinated meat (or vegetables) for a few minutes until it starts to release aroma and partially cooks.
  5. Add a layer of partially cooked rice over the meat. Sprinkle fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk (or food color). Repeat layers if needed.
  6. Cover the pot with a tight lid (or seal with dough) and cook on low heat for 20–30 minutes. This allows the flavors to blend and the rice to fully cook.
  7. Gently mix the layers before serving. Serve hot with raita, salad, or gravy (salan).
