Pizza Recipe

Pizza is a popular Italian dish made with a soft, baked dough base topped with tomato sauce, melted cheese, and a variety of ingredients such as vegetables, meat, or seafood. It is typically seasoned with herbs like oregano and baked at a high temperature until the crust is crispy and the cheese is bubbly. Loved worldwide, pizza is versatile and can be customized to suit different tastes, making it a favorite comfort food enjoyed in many styles and flavors.

Pizza is prepared by first making or using ready-made dough, which is allowed to rest and rise until soft and elastic. The dough is then rolled out into a flat circular base and placed on a baking tray. A layer of tomato-based pizza sauce is spread evenly over the surface, followed by grated cheese and a selection of toppings such as vegetables, chicken, or paneer.

The prepared pizza is baked in a preheated oven at a high temperature (around 220°C) until the crust becomes golden and the cheese melts and turns slightly bubbly. Once done, it is removed from the oven, sliced, and often garnished with oregano or chili flakes before being served hot.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (flour, yeast, water, salt, oil)
  • Pizza sauce (tomato sauce with herbs)
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Toppings (onions, capsicum, mushrooms, olives, chicken, paneer, etc.)
  • Oregano and chili flakes
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Prepare or buy pizza dough and let it rest until it rises.
  2. Roll the dough into a flat base.
  3. Spread pizza sauce evenly over the base.
  4. Add a layer of mozzarella cheese.
  5. Place your choice of toppings.
  6. Sprinkle oregano, chili flakes, and a little olive oil.
  7. Bake in a preheated oven at around 220°C for 12–15 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese melts.
  8. Slice and serve hot.
